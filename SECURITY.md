# Security Policy

Thanks for helping keep OnlineSched and the people who use it safe. We really do
appreciate anyone who takes the time to report a problem responsibly.

A little context so we're all on the same page: OnlineSched is built and run by
volunteers at a non-profit. We made it primarily for our own convention, Furry
Migration, and we share it freely because we'd love other groups to use it too.
That also means our attention right now is mostly on our own site. You're very
welcome to run OnlineSched yourself, but please know that support and fixes come
from a small volunteer crew working in their spare time. We'll always take
security reports seriously — we just may not be as fast as a full-time company,
and we wanted to be honest about that up front.

## Reporting a vulnerability

If you think you've found a security issue, **please don't open a public
issue.** A public report can tell the wrong people how to attack sites that are
still running the plugin before we've had a chance to fix it.

Instead, report it privately through GitHub:

1. Go to the [Security tab](https://github.com/minnesota-furs/OnlineSched/security)
   of the repository.
2. Click **Report a vulnerability**.
3. Tell us what you found.

That opens a private conversation with the maintainers that no one else can see.

If you can, it helps a lot to include:

- What the problem is, in plain language.
- The steps to reproduce it (a proof of concept is great, but not required).
- Which version of OnlineSched you were using.
- Anything you think we should know about the impact.

You don't need to have all of that figured out. A rough report from someone who
noticed something off is far better than silence. Send what you have.

## What happens next

- We'll do our best to acknowledge your report within a few days. We're
  volunteers, so please be patient if it takes a little longer.
- We'll look into it, keep you in the loop, and let you know once there's a fix.
- If you'd like credit for the find, tell us how you'd like to be named. If
  you'd rather stay anonymous, that's completely fine too.

## Supported versions

We provide security fixes for the **latest released version** of OnlineSched.
If you're running an older release, the first step is almost always to update to
the current version.

## Please don't

- Don't run tests against sites you don't own or have permission to test.
- Don't access, change, or delete data that isn't yours.
- Don't publicly disclose the issue until we've had a reasonable chance to fix
  it and get a release out.

Report in good faith and give us a fair chance to respond, and we'll treat your
report the same way. Thank you for looking out for the community. 🐾
